Written from five seats: **BDM, Marketing, SEO, Designer, Developer, Tester**.
No sugar-coating. Verified against live site + source code.

## TL;DR — Why you are not ranking

You are doing a *lot* of the right technical things (SSR, schema, llms.txt,
security headers, hreflang, IndexNow). The reason you are not in Google's top
results for national / global queries is **not** "we forgot a meta tag." It is
three structural problems plus a handful of credibility-killing bugs:

1. **You look like a doorway-page operator.** ~1,628 city × service pages are
   95% identical, generated from `cityServiceAutoContent.ts` with hash-based
   shuffling. Google's "scaled content abuse" policy (March 2024) targets
   exactly this. It can suppress *every* page on your domain — including the
   good ones.
2. **You broadcast that you are not who you say you are.** The "Trusted by"
   strip on the homepage shows Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Netflix, Meta,
   Spotify, Stripe, Adobe, etc. Unless these are actual clients with proof,
   this is an E-E-A-T (and arguably FTC) violation. A Google quality rater or
   a buyer who clicks through will downrank you instantly.
3. **You have no real authority signal for "global / national."** No
   Wikipedia entity, no notable backlinks called out, no PR mentions, no real
   review feed (the AggregateRating "4.9 / 352 reviews" is hard-coded JSON-LD
   with no review platform behind it — Google can detect that). For "digital
   marketing agency" globally you are competing with 20-year-old domains with
   thousands of editorial backlinks. There is no shortcut here.

Plus there are real, verifiable bugs (next section). Fix those first — they
are cheap and visible.

## Part 1 — Bugs I confirmed (most are < 1 hour to fix)

### BUG-1 (Critical, SEO) — `<title>` says "TML Agency | TML Agency"

`src/app/layout.tsx` defines a title template:

```ts
title: { default: "...", template: "%s | TML Agency" }
```

Then 13 child pages set their own `title` already containing "TML Agency",
so Next.js appends "| TML Agency" again. Live verification:

- `/digital-marketing-agency-chandigarh/` →
  **"Digital Marketing Agency in Chandigarh | TML Agency | TML Agency"**

Affected files (all set `title: "... | TML Agency"`):
`src/app/blog/page.tsx`, `case-studies/page.tsx`, `industries/page.tsx`,
`free-tools/page.tsx`, `locations/page.tsx`, `portfolio/page.tsx`,
`authors/page.tsx`, `privacy-policy/page.tsx`, `terms-of-service/page.tsx`,
`digital-marketing-agency-chandigarh/page.tsx`, and the per-slug pages under
those routes.

**Fix:** either (a) remove " | TML Agency" from every per-page `title`, or
(b) in `layout.tsx` use `template: "%s"` and add the brand suffix manually
where needed, or (c) use `title: { absolute: "..." }` on the per-page metadata.

### BUG-2 (Critical, runtime) — `Math.random()` called during render

`src/components/sections/VideoShowcase.tsx` lines 28–37 call `Math.random()`
inside `useRef(...)` initializer in the render body. With React 19 strict
mode and SSR this produces **hydration mismatches**: server HTML uses
different particle positions than the client, React throws a hydration
warning, and Lighthouse penalises CLS / TBT.

ESLint flags it 5 times under `react-hooks/purity`. Fix with a stable seed
or `useState(() => …)` inside `useEffect`:

```tsx
const [particles, setParticles] = useState<Particle[]>([]);
useEffect(() => {
  setParticles(Array.from({ length: 8 }, (_, i) => ({ /* random */ })));
}, []);
```

### BUG-3 (High, SEO) — H1 on `/services/seo/` is literally "SEO."

That is the single highest-priority service page and its H1 is one word and
a period. Should be the keyword you want to rank for, e.g.
*"SEO Services for Brands That Want to Rank in 2026"* or
*"SEO Agency in India — Data-Driven Organic Growth"*.

### BUG-4 (High, trust) — "Trusted by" shows brands you almost certainly do not work with

GOOGLE, MICROSOFT, AMAZON, SHOPIFY, HUBSPOT, META, SPOTIFY, STRIPE, SLACK,
ADOBE, NETFLIX, AIRBNB.

Either rename the section to **"Tools & platforms we work with"** (honest)
or replace with **real client logos** (from your actual case studies — Luxe
Interiors, CB Builders, TechVault, etc. that already appear in
testimonials). Mixing the two is the worst option for trust.

### BUG-5 (High, performance) — Templates are still `"use client"`

`LocationServiceTemplate.tsx` (1,278 lines), `ServicePageTemplate.tsx`
(981 lines), and `ChandigarhServiceTemplate.tsx` (486 lines) all start with
`"use client"`. Even though `LocationServicePage.tsx` now passes prepared
data, the entire template tree still ships to the browser as JS. This is
why the previous audit measured 29,900 ms TBT and Lighthouse mobile 37.

Most of these templates are static content with two or three interactive
islands (counter, in-view animation). Convert the page shell to a Server
Component and isolate the animated bits into small client wrappers.

### BUG-6 (Medium) — ESLint hygiene

`npm run lint` reports **10 errors, 55 warnings**. Beyond BUG-2 above:

- `src/data/cityServiceAutoContent.ts:45` uses `any`
- Several unused imports / vars in `noindex.ts`, `serviceFeatureVariants.ts`,
  `WorkHome2.tsx`, etc.

These are not breaking the build, but they signal an unmaintained codebase
to anyone (or any AI) doing due diligence on you.

### BUG-7 (Medium) — Hard-coded `AggregateRating 4.9 / 352 reviews`

`src/lib/schema.ts` line 228 outputs the same rating on every page. If you
do not have 352 reviews on a verifiable platform (Google Business Profile,
Trustpilot, Clutch), Google will treat this as fake and may issue a
manual-action review-snippet penalty. This is a known 2024-2025 enforcement
trend.

### BUG-8 (Medium, UX) — No Google Maps embed on local pages

`/digital-marketing-agency-chandigarh/` has a Google Maps **link** but no
embedded iframe and no GBP Place ID in the schema. Local pack ranking lifts
significantly when the embed + Place ID are present.

### BUG-9 (Low) — Build cannot finish in this sandbox

`build.log` shows the last build failed because of a stale `.next/lock`,
and `rm -rf .next` here fails on permission. This is a local environment
issue but worth a clean reset before your next deploy.

## Part 2 — Why you are not ranking on Google (the strategic answer)

I am separating *what* from *why*. Most of what is below is hard truth.

### The structural problems

**Problem A — Scaled programmatic content.**
You have ~1,800 city × service permutation pages produced by
`cityServiceAutoContent.ts`. 148 of 158 cities have **no** `uniqueContent`.
They share an 8-template H1 pool and randomly shuffle industries on each
page. Google's helpful-content classifier evaluates **the whole domain**.
Even if your homepage and Chandigarh page are good, ~85% of your indexable
URLs are flagged as low-value, which drags everything down.

What to do: noindex every URL whose `uniqueContent` field is empty. Reduce
the supported cities from 158 to the 15-25 you can actually defend with
case studies. Be willing to delete pages.

**Problem B — No real authority for global queries.**
"Digital marketing agency" globally is dominated by Ogilvy, WPP, R/GA,
Wieden+Kennedy, Dentsu, plus Indian-focused leaders like WebChutney, Dentsu
Webchutney, Schbang, and Foxymoron. They have:

- Wikipedia entries
- Editorial mentions in *Adweek, Campaign, AdAge*
- Hundreds of dofollow backlinks from `.edu`/`.gov` and major media
- Industry association memberships (IAMAI, AAAI)
- Clutch / G2 profiles with 100+ verified reviews

You have none of these surfaced. Until you do, you will not rank for
generic global terms. You **can** rank for:

- Long-tail intent: *"branding agency for fintech in India"*, *"AI
  influencer marketing agency"*
- Local: *"digital marketing agency Chandigarh"*, *"SEO Mohali"*
- Category-specific: *"music release marketing"* (you have a service page
  for it; very low competition)

**Problem C — No E-E-A-T person.**
Author pages return 404. Founder titles contradict between homepage and
about page (Owner & Founder vs SEO & Growth vs Visionary). Google's 2024
rater guidelines weight individual author authority heavily for YMYL and
service businesses. Build out two real founder pages with photos, LinkedIn
links, speaking engagements, podcast appearances, contributed articles.

**Problem D — Batch-published blog dates.**
295 blog posts cluster in March-April 2026. This pattern matches AI-
generated content batches. Even if a human wrote them, the date
distribution looks suspicious. Stagger publication dates and keep a
weekly cadence going forward.

**Problem E — Title and headline bugs (Part 1) cap CTR.**
Even if you ranked, "Digital Marketing Agency in Chandigarh | TML Agency |
TML Agency" loses the click. CTR is a ranking factor (indirect, but real).

### What is genuinely working — keep doing this

- Full SSR. Google can render every page first try.
- llms.txt + llms-full.txt — you are ahead of 95% of agencies on AI
  citation readiness.
- Robots.txt with named AI bot allowances — best practice.
- IndexNow file — instant Bing notifications.
- Security headers (HSTS preload, CSP-Report-Only) — A+ grade.
- Sitemap trailing slashes are now consistent with `trailingSlash: true`
  (the previous audit's #1 critical issue is **fixed**).
- Schema foundation (single ProfessionalService now — duplicate fixed).
- Image pipeline (Next/Image with AVIF/WebP, srcSet, lazy).
- Real contact form with budget tier and service routing.

## Part 3 — Findings by team seat

### Marketing team
- **Positioning is muddled.** Homepage H1 "Build brands. Drive growth." is a
  slogan, not a search-intent phrase. Sub-head says "full-service branding
  & marketing agency for ambitious businesses" — also slogan. Move
  *"Digital marketing agency in India serving 500+ brands across 6
  countries"* into the H1 or H2 area.
- **No reason-to-believe.** "500+ Brands · 15+ Years · 98% Retention" with
  no logo proof, no client list page (just a "Trusted by" strip with
  non-clients), no published case-study results.
- **Case studies exist but are buried.** `/case-studies/` is in the sitemap;
  link to specific case studies from the homepage hero with hard numbers
  ("3x ROAS in 90 days for Luxe Interiors").
- **Missing channel mix.** A digital marketing agency that does not show
  its own paid funnel, retargeting, or email capture is a credibility hit.
- **No lead magnets.** `/free-tools/` exists but is not promoted. The blog
  has a newsletter mention but no signup form on the listing page.

### BDM (Business Development) team
- **Pricing is invisible.** B2B buyers shortlist on price tier. Add
  retainer ranges (₹X – ₹Y / month per service) on each service page. You
  already collect budget in the form, so you know the tiers.
- **No "Book a call" friction reduction.** Calendly link is present on the
  Chandigarh page but not on the global homepage. Put it in the nav.
- **Logo wall problem (see BUG-4).** A buyer who recognizes the Stripe or
  Netflix logo will demand to see the campaign. When you can't produce
  one, the deal dies.
- **No proposal / pitch-deck CTA.** Add "Download our credentials deck"
  for procurement workflows.
- **No Clutch / GoodFirms presence surfaced.** Most agency RFPs require a
  Clutch profile. Build one and link to it.

### SEO team
- **The 1,628 thin geo pages problem (Problem A above) is the #1 risk.**
- **Title-template bug across 13+ pages (BUG-1).**
- **H1 = "SEO." on the most important service page (BUG-3).**
- **Hard-coded AggregateRating (BUG-7).**
- **Internal linking is poor.** Service pages don't link laterally to
  related services or to relevant blog posts. Add 4-6 contextual internal
  links per page.
- **Author bylines missing on service pages.** Add a "Reviewed by Raman
  Makkar, Founder & Head of SEO" block with link to author page.
- **No Wikipedia entity.** This is the highest-ROI single move for AI
  Overviews citation.
- **Blog post dates are batch-clustered.** Stagger them.
- **No alt-text audit at scale on the geo pages.**

### Designer / UX team
- **Homepage hero is a video that delays LCP.** Hero LCP measured 3.6 s.
  Put a static webp poster *first*, swap in the video on intersection.
  Add `<link rel="preload" as="image" href="/hero-poster.webp">`.
- **Black background + low-contrast secondary text** in places — run an
  axe-core / Lighthouse accessibility pass; AA contrast ratio failures
  hurt both rankings (Page Experience signal) and conversions.
- **CTA buttons are inconsistent.** Some say "Get Started", some "Get a
  Free Quote", some "Get a Free Strategy Call", some "Book Free Strategy
  Call". Pick one verb-noun pair and use it everywhere.
- **The particles animation in VideoShowcase** is low-value visual noise
  and a hydration risk (BUG-2). Remove it.
- **Mobile menu and inner-nav** were not stress-tested in this audit but
  the inner-nav component imports look heavy. Lighthouse mobile 37 on geo
  pages will hurt every UX metric.
- **No skeleton states** for the blog listing — flickers as React mounts.

### Developer team
- **`"use client"` on giant template files (BUG-5).** Single biggest perf
  win available.
- **Data files (`blogArticles.ts` 14k lines, `industries.ts` 4k lines,
  `cityServiceContent.ts` 4k lines) imported into client bundles** — even
  tree-shaken these inflate JS payload. Move to JSON loaded on the server
  or to a content layer (MDX, Sanity, Contentlayer, or just file-system).
- **`Math.random()` in render (BUG-2).**
- **ESLint errors blocking eventual `eslint --max-warnings 0` (BUG-6).**
- **`google-service-account.json` is committed at repo root** — verify
  it's gitignored; if not, this is a credentials leak. Even if local-only,
  it should not sit beside `package.json`.
- **No CI mentioned in the docs.** Add Lighthouse CI on PRs to catch perf
  regressions. Add `next build && next lint --max-warnings 0` as a
  required check.
- **TypeScript passes (`tsc --noEmit` clean).** Good.

### Tester / QA team
What I would test next, in order:
1. Crawl every URL with Screaming Frog or Sitebulb. Count:
   - 308/301 chains, 404s, soft 404s, duplicate titles, duplicate H1s,
     missing meta descriptions.
2. Run Lighthouse CI against the 10 most important URLs (homepage, /about,
   /services/seo, /services/google-ads, /services/website-development,
   /digital-marketing-agency-chandigarh, /portfolio, /contact, /blog, one
   geo page).
3. Run Schema.org validator (`validator.schema.org`) on those 10. Confirm
   no duplicate @id, no missing required fields, no broken Article or
   FAQPage.
4. Check Google Search Console:
   - "Pages indexed" vs "Pages not indexed" — if not-indexed > indexed,
     you have a quality signal problem.
   - "Crawled - currently not indexed" — this is the Helpful Content
     signal showing.
   - "Manual actions" — confirm none.
5. Check Bing Webmaster Tools (often forgotten — and Bing powers ChatGPT
   web search and DuckDuckGo).
6. Submit the site to Ahrefs / Semrush free site audit; export the issues
   list and prioritise.
7. Lighthouse accessibility pass — fix any contrast / aria-label failures.
8. Run a real form submission end-to-end and confirm the lead lands
   somewhere a human reads (Slack, CRM, email).

## Part 5 — What an honest 90-day plan looks like

**Month 1 — Stop the bleeding.**
Items 1-9 above. Audit Search Console for soft-404s and indexation. Pull
the doorway pages out of Google's index. Fix the title and H1 bugs. Replace
the fake-looking logo wall.

**Month 2 — Build defensible authority.**
Two cornerstone case studies with real client names, photos, numbers.
Founder author pages live with LinkedIn proof. Three founder podcast guest
appearances booked. First HARO / Qwoted responses sent. Clutch profile live
with 5+ verified reviews.

**Month 3 — Earn citation mass.**
Original research piece (e.g. "We analysed 500 Indian D2C brands' Google
Ads spend in 2026") — pitched to Inc42, YourStory, Adgully. Three guest
posts on tier-2 marketing publications. Review the geo-page strategy: keep
top 25, kill the rest. Republish the strongest 20 blog posts with new
dates and updated 2026 data.

After 90 days you will not be #1 globally. You will have stopped looking
like a programmatic content farm and started looking like a credible
agency that earns rankings. That is the only version of this that lasts.
